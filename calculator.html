The Point: Even modest fee differences—0.5% vs 0.19%—compound into massive wealth differences over decades. This illustration is deliberately simple: it assumes flat 6% growth and ignores TERs, tax structures, and alpha generation.

The Reality: Active asset managers often carry hidden costs. Exotic fund TERs frequently run 1-3% annually—far higher than passive ETFs (0.05-0.3%). Some skilled advisors and managers generate returns exceeding their fees, creating net value. Others don't. This illustration doesn't solve that question.

For Whom This Matters Most: Investors who believe (a) passive index investing wins, (b) they can pick securities as well as any manager, or (c) they want to minimize fee drag. If you value professional guidance and believe your advisor generates alpha, the calculus changes entirely—and may justify the cost. There's no one-size-fits-all answer.

Full Disclaimer: This calculator is a teaching tool and does NOT constitute investment advice. This simplified illustration assumes a flat 6% annual growth rate and deliberately excludes many critical factors: (1) Total Expense Ratios (TERs) of active funds, which often run 1-3% annually for exotic assets versus 0.05-0.3% for passive ETFs, (2) Tax-efficient investment structures and instruments that may reduce effective fees, (3) The potential for skilled advisors or asset managers to generate alpha (returns exceeding their fees), (4) Individual risk profiles, time horizons, and personal circumstances. Some investors benefit significantly from professional financial guidance and may generate returns that exceed advisory fees. This illustration simply demonstrates that fee drag compounds meaningfully over decades—particularly relevant for investors who believe passive investing is sufficient or that they can select investments as effectively as professional managers. Always consult with a qualified financial advisor regarding your specific situation before making investment decisions.
